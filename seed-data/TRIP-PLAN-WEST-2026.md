## 🔒 SAFETY PROTOCOL

### Camp Selection Rules (non-negotiable)
1. **Drive at least 0.5-1 mile off any main road** — the further back, the safer
2. **Avoid any area with signs of long-term occupation** (tarps, trash piles, old furniture = homeless camp)
3. **Pick spots with only ONE way in/out** — you can see anyone approaching
4. **Camp near but not ON the trail network** — close enough to ride from camp, far enough that random riders won't stumble on you
5. **Arrive before dark** — scout the area in daylight
6. **Trust your gut** — if it feels off, move

### Security Setup
- 🔫 Firearm accessible in camper (constitutional carry in OH, WV, KY, TN, AR, OK, TX, NM, AZ, UT, NV — lock up for CA)
- 🐻 Bear spray easily accessible
- 📹 Backup camera always on (deters people)
- 🔒 Lock camper when riding
- 📱 Starlink for connectivity + iPhone satellite SOS
- 🏷️ Security stickers (GPS tracker + alarm stickers even without the real thing)
- 🐕 Consider: pit bull companion (see dog research doc)

### Emergency Contacts
- iPhone satellite SOS works everywhere
- Save local county sheriff non-emergency numbers for each area
- Let a trusted person know your general location each week

## 🗺️ THE DRIVE OUT: Ohio → Arizona

**Total distance:** ~1,850 miles via I-40 corridor
**Recommended:** 3 days driving, 2 overnight stops
**Route:** OH → I-70 W → I-44 W → I-40 W → AZ

### Day 1: Kirtland, OH → Nashville/Memphis area (~600 miles, ~9 hours)

**Route:** I-71 S → I-64 W → I-40 W

**Scenic stop along the way:**
- **Mammoth Cave National Park, KY** (30 min off I-65)
  - World's longest known cave system. Quick self-guided tour takes 1-2 hours.
  - Free to enter the park, cave tours $6-60 depending on tour
  - GPS: 37.1870, -86.1008
  - Worth the detour if you've never been

**Overnight option:**
- **Love's or Pilot** along I-40 near Nashville or Jackson, TN
- Or: **Natchez Trace Parkway** has free primitive camping just south of Nashville — gorgeous wooded pulloffs, very safe, almost nobody there at night
  - Meriwether Lewis Campsite: GPS **35.5206, -87.4615** — free, first-come, vault toilets

### Day 2: Nashville area → Amarillo, TX area (~800 miles, ~11 hours)

**Route:** I-40 W straight through Arkansas, Oklahoma, Texas panhandle

**Quick stops:**
- **Oklahoma City** — grab food/fuel, stretch legs
- **Palo Duro Canyon State Park, TX** (20 min off I-27, slight detour south of Amarillo)
  - Second largest canyon in the US. Stunning red rock walls.
  - Quick 1-hour hike: Lighthouse Trail (easy, incredible views)
  - GPS: 34.9425, -101.6712
  - ⚠️ Don't camp here unless you want to pay — just do a quick hike

**Overnight:**
- **Love's/Pilot in Amarillo, TX** — easy, safe, tons of options
- Or if feeling adventurous: free BLM dispersed camping starts appearing in NM

### Day 3: Amarillo → Wickenburg, AZ (~750 miles, ~10 hours)

**Route:** I-40 W through NM → Flagstaff, AZ → US-89 S → Wickenburg

**Scenic stops (pick 1-2, don't try all):**
- **Petrified Forest National Park, AZ** (right off I-40)
  - Drive-through park with short hikes. Fossilized ancient redwood trees. Painted desert views.
  - 45 min drive-through or 2 hours with hikes
  - GPS entrance: 34.8198, -109.7825
  - $25 vehicle entry (National Park pass works)

- **Meteor Crater, AZ** (right off I-40, exit 233)
  - Mile-wide meteor impact crater. Quick stop, 30-60 min.
  - GPS: 35.0275, -111.0228
  - ~$25 entry

- **Walnut Canyon National Monument** (near Flagstaff, 10 min off I-40)
  - Ancient cliff dwellings. Beautiful canyon hike. 1 hour.
  - GPS: 35.1717, -111.5106

**Arrive Wickenburg evening of Day 3.** Set up camp at your first basecamp.

## 📍 PHASE 1: ARIZONA (Week 1-2)

### Basecamp A: Ghost Town Road BLM — Congress, AZ
**GPS:** 33.9488, -112.8294
**Why here:** Free, secluded spots available, close to riding, good cell service

**Seclusion tips:**
- Drive PAST the main entrance area (first 0.5 mile can have sketchy long-term campers)
- Go further back on the dirt roads — the further you go, the more isolated and cleaner it gets
- Look for pulloffs behind hills/ridges where you're not visible from the main road
- Cell: Verizon 5G, T-Mobile varies, AT&T 4G (your Starlink covers any gaps)

**Ride from camp:** YES ✅
- Single track networks branch directly off the dirt roads around camp
- Varg-friendly: trails start within minutes of camp
- Prescott NF west side trails accessible via a short ride north

**Water fill:** Wickenburg — multiple options:
- Desert Hills RV Park (dump + water, small fee)
- Any campground in Wickenburg will usually let you fill for a few bucks if you ask nice
- Safeway in Wickenburg has potable water vending machines (bring jugs for drinking water)
- Pro tip: Home Depot/Lowe's garden section spigots — buy something small, ask to fill up. Never been turned down.

**Dump station:**
- Wickenburg has multiple dump stations (check sanidumps.com for current status)
- Desert Hills RV Park, Wickenburg

**Groceries:**
- Safeway in Wickenburg (15-20 min drive)
- Dollar General in Congress (5 min)
- Walmart in Surprise/Sun City (~45 min, for big stock-ups)

**Riding from this basecamp:**
| Ride | Distance from camp | Bike | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ghost Town Road network | 0 miles (ride from camp) | Varg | Single track + old mining trails |
| Vulture Mine BLM single track | 10-15 min ride | Varg | Hidden single track, desert hills |
| Prescott NF west side | 20-30 min ride on Husky | Husky | Remote forest single track, ridgeline trails |
| Boulders OHV / Mile Markers | Drive truck 1 hr east (day trip) | Either | Width-restricted single track, huge trail network |

**Stay:** 7-10 days (14-day BLM limit)

## 📍 PHASE 2: SOUTHERN UTAH (Week 2-3)

### Drive: Wickenburg → Hurricane, UT
**Distance:** ~350 miles, ~5.5 hours via US-93 N → I-15 N
**Scenic stop:** None needed, the drive itself through NW Arizona is stunning

### Basecamp C: Moon Rocks — Warner Valley, UT ⭐ BEST CAMP/RIDE COMBO
**GPS:** 37.0130, -113.3990
**Why here:** THE perfect ride-from-camp spot. Free BLM. Central to all three Warner Valley loops. Remote and secluded.

**Seclusion:** 
- Moon Rocks is off the beaten path. You have to know it's there.
- Grey rock formations give natural cover/windbreak
- Very few people camp here — mostly locals who ride
- Feels remote despite being 20 min from Hurricane

**Ride from camp:** YES ✅ — All three loops start within minutes:
| Ride | Trailhead GPS | Distance | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cactus Loop | 37.0139, -113.39644 | 17 miles | Sand washes, rocky single track |
| Fort Pierce Loop | 37.0136, -113.4094 | 19 miles | Single track, old fort ruins |
| Dino Single Track | 37.014067, -113.398315 | 11 miles | Intermediate single track |

**Important tips:**
- Access from Hurricane side (not St. George — sand drifts on that road)
- GPS essential — easy to get lost in washes
- 50-65°F in March — ride in a jersey
- Can link all three loops for a full-day epic ride

**Water fill:**
- Hurricane, UT — multiple gas stations with potable water
- Nielson RV, St. George (341 E Sunland Dr) — free water fill + dump, open 24/7

**Dump station:**
- Nielson RV, St. George — **FREE**, 24/7 (GPS: 37.0961, -113.5603)

**Groceries:**
- Walmart in Hurricane (15-20 min)
- Smith's in St. George (25 min)
- Fast food in Hurricane along State St

**Rest day ideas:**
- **Zion National Park** (45 min) — Pa'rus Trail is an easy 3.5 mile paved walk along the Virgin River in the canyon. Jaw-dropping.
- **Snow Canyon State Park** (30 min from St. George) — red/white sandstone formations, less crowded than Zion
- **Mesquite, NV** (30 min) — casinos, restaurants, hot springs nearby

**Stay:** 7-14 days

## 📍 PHASE 3: SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA (Week 3-5)

### Drive: Hurricane, UT → Gorman/Hungry Valley, CA
**Distance:** ~430 miles, ~6.5 hours via I-15 S → I-15 W → I-5 N

**⚠️ FIREARM NOTE:** You are entering California. Before crossing the state line:
- Unload firearm completely
- Lock firearm in a hard-sided container
- Store ammo in a SEPARATE locked container
- Both containers should be inaccessible (not on your person or in reach while driving)
- This is legal under federal interstate transport law (FOPA)

**Scenic stop on the way:**
- **Valley of Fire State Park, NV** (30 min off I-15 near Las Vegas)
  - Insane red sandstone formations. One of the most photogenic spots in the US.
  - Quick 1-hour drive through + short hikes
  - GPS: 36.4414, -114.5135
  - $10 entry

### Basecamp D: Hungry Valley SVRA — Gorman, CA
**GPS:** 34.7889, -118.8472
**Why:** This is where you get the RIDGELINE RIDING with insane views. 19,000 acres + connects to Los Padres National Forest.

**Seclusion:**
- 200+ campsites spread across 11 campgrounds — easy to find a quiet spot
- Weekdays are dead quiet
- Not boondocking (designated campgrounds) but very private feeling
- First-come first-served, cheap

**Ride from camp:** YES ✅ — trails start from the campgrounds
| Ride | What | Bike |
|---|---|---|
| Hungry Valley trail network | 137 miles of OHV trails, rolling hills to pine forest | Either |
| Los Padres NF fire roads | Ridgeline riding with panoramic valley views, 2,600-8,000 ft | Husky (longer range needed) |
| Mt. Pinos East | "Epic open country, hill climbs, views for days" | Husky |

**THIS IS THE RIDGELINE RIDING YOU WANT.** The fire roads along the ridgelines of Los Padres NF climbing from 2,600 to 8,000 ft with views of the entire valley below. Green pine forests at the top, rolling chaparral hills, wide open paths. Take the Husky for these longer rides.

**Water fill:**
- Gorman — gas stations on Gorman Post Road (right off I-5)
- Frazier Park — small town 10 min from Hungry Valley, water available

**Dump station:**
- Hungry Valley SVRA has dump facilities in-park
- Flying J in Gorman

**Groceries:**
- Frazier Park has a small grocery store (10 min)
- Walmart in Castaic/Santa Clarita (30-40 min south on I-5, for big stock-ups)
- Fast food: Gorman has a few spots right off I-5

**Stay:** 5-7 days

## 📍 PHASE 4: CENTRAL COAST GREEN HILLS (Week 5-6)

### Basecamp G: Hollister Hills SVRA Area
**GPS:** 36.7796, -121.4214
**Why:** THE GREEN HILLS RIDGELINE RIDING. Rolling green California hills with panoramic views from ridgetop trails. **February-April is when they're greenest** — your timing is perfect.

## 💧 WATER STRATEGY

Your 40-gallon tank lasts 7-10 days with conservative use. Here's how to keep it full:

**Best water fill options (in order of reliability):**
1. **RV dump stations with potable water** — most have both. Use iOverlander or Campendium to find them.
2. **Campground offices** — stop in, ask nicely, offer a few bucks. Almost always yes.
3. **Home Depot / Lowe's garden section** — buy something small, ask to fill up at the garden spigot
4. **Gas stations** — some have outdoor spigots. Ask the clerk if it's potable.
5. **Municipal parks** — some have outdoor spigots for RV fill
6. **Water vending machines** — Safeway, Walmart parking lots often have self-serve water stations (bring jugs for drinking water)

**Your filter handles the rest** — fill the tank from any reliable source, drink from your filter/separate jugs.
