# 🚐 Western Trip Research — Late Winter/Spring 2026

**Goal:** Open-ended trip (3+ weeks, possibly months) out west. Single track + ridgeline/open hill riding + boondocking. Warm weather focus. Possible PNW extension when spring arrives.

**Rig:** 2023 F350 DRW 7.3L gas + Northern Lite 10-2 EXCDLE truck camper. Dirt bikes on front/rear hitch carriers (Stark Varg EX + Husqvarna FE 501S). Off-grid: solar, lead batteries, generator, power bank, Starlink.

**Preferences:** Green mountains/hills with views > desert mountains/hills > flat (hates flat). Single track focused + wants some wide open ridgeline/hilltop riding with panoramic views. Boondocking/BLM preferred. Avoid crowds.

---

## 🗺️ RECOMMENDED ROUTE

**Ohio → I-40 West → AZ → UT → CA → (PNW when weather warms)**

Drive: ~28 hours / 1,900 miles Ohio to Wickenburg. Do it in 2-3 days.

### 1. 🌵 WICKENBURG, AZ — BLM Single Track Paradise
**Your proven spot. But there's way more here than most people know.**

#### Exact Riding Areas:

**A) Vulture Mine Road BLM Network**
- GPS staging: **33.8645, -112.7812** (Vulture Mine Road pulloff)
- What: Unmarked single track carved by decades of local riders through Sonoran desert. Rocky, technical, rolling hills with saguaros everywhere.
- The trails are NOT on any map. You find them by exploring — look for worn paths branching off the dirt roads. OnX Offroad will show some as "unnamed trails."
- Difficulty: Intermediate-Advanced
- Tips: Ride the ridgelines heading west toward Prescott NF boundary. The terrain gets hillier and more interesting.

**B) Prescott National Forest — West Side MVUM Single Track**
- **This is the real hidden gem.** Download the Prescott NF Motor Vehicle Use Map (west side): https://www.fs.usda.gov/detailfull/prescott/maps-pubs/?cid=fseprd585923
- Every dotted line on the MVUM = legal single track for motorcycles
- GPS area: roughly **34.15, -112.60** (west of Yarnell)
- Terrain: Transitions from desert to chaparral to pine forest as elevation rises. Way more varied than pure desert riding.
- REMOTE. GPS mandatory. Spotty cell service. Bring water, tools, spare tube.
- Best approach: Drive north from Wickenburg on US-89 toward Yarnell/Congress, access forest roads heading east

**C) Ghost Town Road Network**
- GPS staging: **33.95, -112.75** (near Congress, AZ)
- Mix of old mining roads and trails. Less crowded than Vulture Mine area.
- Some interesting abandoned mine structures (stay out of shafts obviously)

#### Exact Boondocking Spots:

**⭐ Ghost Town Road Dispersed — RECOMMENDED**
- GPS: **33.9488, -112.8294**
- Campendium: https://www.campendium.com/ghost-town-road
- Free, BLM, 14-day limit
- Recent review (Feb 2026): "Very nice and clean"
- Truck camper review (Apr 2025): "Spent 13 days here. Found a nice level spot away from main entrance. Secluded enough to feel alone."
- Cell: Verizon 5G, T-Mobile 5G, AT&T 4G
- Supplies: Gas station, dollar store, grocery store 5 min away in Congress. Wickenburg 20 min.
- ⚠️ Warning: Mixed reviews — some report homeless camps near entrance. Drive further back for privacy and better spots. Avoid after heavy rain (silty mud).

**⭐ Vulture Peak State Trust Land — RECOMMENDED**
- GPS: **33.8358, -112.8134**
- Campendium: https://www.campendium.com/vulture-peak
- Requires **$15/year AZ State Trust Land permit** — buy at https://land.az.gov/recreational-permits
- Verizon cell tower literally across the street — excellent signal
- Tons of room, easy to level, great sunsets
- ⚠️ Warning: Road in is rutted/rough but doable with any truck. Some trash in spots.

**❌ Box Wash BLM — CLOSED**
- Was a great spot but has been closed since 2022 for construction. May reopen as a paid campground.
- DO NOT PLAN ON THIS SPOT.

**Rincon Road BLM**
- GPS: approx **33.90, -112.67**
- Free, less known, good Verizon signal
- Near Wickenburg for supplies

### 2. 🏜️ BOULDERS OHV / MILE MARKERS — NW of Phoenix
**Hundreds of miles of trails. Designated single track sections. Camp right at the staging area.**

#### Exact Riding:

**Boulders OHV Staging Area**
- GPS: **33.8798, -112.3055**
- Over 200 miles of trails through Upper Sonoran Desert
- **Mile Markers single track** — bike-only width-restricted trails throughout the area. Look for "width restricted" signs = single track only.
- Rolling hills, washes, boulder fields, saguaros
- Easy to advanced sections

**Wildcat Pass Area (NE of Phoenix)**
- GPS staging: approx **33.80, -111.78**
- "Nice flowy hardpack sections, mix of singletrack, width-restricted, and single lane dirt roads"
- Less crowded than Boulders

#### Exact Boondocking:

**⭐ Boulders OHV Area — Camp right there**
- GPS: Same as staging area — **33.8798, -112.3055**
- Free dispersed camping within the OHV area
- Campendium: https://www.campendium.com/boulders-ohv-area
- Truck campers, toy haulers all welcome
- ⚠️ Warning: Can be packed on weekends with OHV traffic. Weekdays are chill.

### 3. 🔴 WARNER VALLEY — St. George/Hurricane, UT
**One of the best winter riding destinations in the US. Rideable in a jersey even in January.**

#### Exact Riding (with GPS):

**Cactus Loop**
- Trailhead GPS: **37.0139, -113.39644**
- 17.1 miles, local favorite
- Sand washes, rocky single track, red rock scenery
- 1-2 hours

**Fort Pierce Loop**
- Trailhead GPS: **37.0136, -113.4094**
- 19 miles, starts near Fort Pierce ruins (old stone fort — cool to explore)
- Mix of washes, single track, desert terrain

**Dino Single Track**
- Trailhead GPS: **37.014067, -113.398315**
- 11 miles, near actual dinosaur tracks site
- Good intermediate single track

**Tips:**
- Come from the **Hurricane side** — the St. George road has bad sand drifts
- GPS essential — easy to get lost in washes
- All three loops can be linked for a full day
- 50-65°F in Feb/March — perfect riding weather

#### Exact Boondocking:

**⭐ Moon Rocks — BEST SPOT (camp + ride from same spot)**
- GPS: **37.0130, -113.3990**
- Free BLM dispersed camping
- Grey-colored rock formations — easy to spot
- Central to ALL three trail loops — literally ride from camp
- Quiet, feels remote

**Hurricane Cliffs BLM**
- GPS area: between Hurricane and Virgin, UT
- 56 designated dispersed sites, first-come first-served
- Fire rings provided
- Free

**Rest Day:** Zion National Park is 45 min from Hurricane. Absolutely worth a day trip.

### 4. 🏔️ HUNGRY VALLEY SVRA + LOS PADRES NF — Gorman, CA
**THIS is where you get the ridgeline riding with insane views.**

#### The Riding:

**Hungry Valley SVRA**
- GPS staging: **34.7889, -118.8472** (North Kiosk entrance)
- Address: 5301 Ralphs Ranch Road, Gorman, CA 93243
- 19,000 acres, 137 miles of OHV trails
- **Terrain ranges from 2,600 ft to over 8,000 ft** — rolling hills, arroyos, scrubland, AND steep pine-forested mountains
- Trails rated by difficulty for motorcycles
- Day use fee (~$5)
- Camping in designated campgrounds (200+ sites, first-come first-served)

**Los Padres National Forest (adjoining)**
- Over 150 miles of National Forest OHV routes connect to Hungry Valley
- **Mt. Pinos East** — "Epic if you love open country and rugged terrain. Hill climbs, tight switchbacks, views for days."
- Fire roads along ridgelines with **panoramic views of the valleys below**
- GPS staging for Mt. Pinos: **34.8139, -119.1428**
- 160+ miles of dirt roads and trails in the Mt. Pinos area alone

#### Boondocking:

**Hungry Valley Campgrounds (in-park)**
- 11 semi-developed campgrounds, 200+ sites
- First-come first-served
- Shade ramadas, picnic tables, fire rings, vault toilets
- NOT free but cheap

**Los Padres NF Dispersed**
- Free dispersed camping throughout the national forest
- Many spots along forest roads with mountain views

### 5. 🌿 ROWHER FLAT OHV — Angeles National Forest, CA
**Ridgeline riding with high desert views. An hour from LA but feels remote.**

#### The Riding:

- GPS staging: **34.4350, -118.3980**
- In Angeles National Forest — designated OHV area (no plate needed)
- Hard-packed terrain, single track, wide fire roads, rocky hill climbs
- **"Trails follow the ridgelines, offering great scenic views"**
- Key trails:
  - **Rowher Trail (3N24)** — climbs from staging to ridgelines
  - **Lookout Trail** — moderate steepness, great views
  - **East Walker Canyon Loop** — rocky sections, diverse terrain
- Elevation: 2,500 ft base → 4,000+ ft ridges
- Views of the high desert from ridgetops

#### Boondocking:
- No camping at Rowher Flat itself
- Nearby options in Angeles NF dispersed areas
- Better as a day-ride stop on your route

### 6. 🌊 HOLLISTER HILLS SVRA — Central Coast, CA
**Rolling golden hillsides with ridgeline trails and oak groves. The "green hills" riding you described.**

⚠️ **SEASONAL NOTE:** These hills are GREEN in late winter/early spring (Feb-April) after rains. This is when they look incredible — by summer they're golden/brown. Your timing would be PERFECT.

#### The Riding:

- GPS: **36.7796, -121.4214** (main entrance on Cienega Road, Hollister)
- 6,800 acres, nearly 200 miles of trails
- Mix of single track and fire roads through **rolling green hills**
- **High Trail** — open ridgeline trail with panoramic views
- **Harmony Gate Trail** — climbs past dense oak forests to ridgeline
- Terrain: Rolling hillsides, oaks, chaparral
- Day use fee (~$5)
- 1 hour south of San Jose

#### Boondocking:
- On-site camping available in designated areas
- Nearby dispersed options limited — this is a day-ride destination
- Could base out of BLM land near Hollister or stay at a campground

### 7. 🏜️ ALABAMA HILLS — Lone Pine, CA
**Not for the riding (though there is some). For the CAMPING. Most epic boondocking backdrop in America.**

- GPS: **36.6083, -118.1131** (Movie Flat area)
- BLM land with free dispersed camping (free permit required — get online or at Eastern Sierra Visitor Center)
- Massive boulder formations with Mt. Whitney and the Sierras as backdrop
- Some dirt roads for exploring, not major riding area
- **This is your "rest day" basecamp on the route between SoCal and NorCal**
- Stunning for photos, sunsets, stargazing

---

### 8. 🌴 JOHNSON VALLEY OHV + JUNIPER FLATS — Lucerne Valley, CA
**Massive BLM riding area. Reddit riders specifically recommend Juniper Flats for single track.**

#### The Riding:

- GPS staging: approx **34.35, -116.45** (Johnson Valley)
- Vast open desert riding + single track networks at Juniper Flats
- Desert hills, varied terrain
- ⚠️ **GPS MANDATORY** — people get lost here frequently. Massive area.
- ⚠️ Open mine shafts in the area — stay away from mine tailings
- Rim locks recommended (rocky terrain)

#### Boondocking:
- Free dispersed camping throughout BLM land
- Joshua Tree National Park nearby for rest day exploring

---

### 9. 🌊 CORRAL CANYON + McCAIN VALLEY — San Diego Backcountry, CA
**Great single track with scenic San Diego backcountry views. Warm all winter.**

- GPS: approx **32.70, -116.35** (Corral Canyon OHV)
- Cleveland National Forest, 45 min east of San Diego
- Single track + fire roads through chaparral-covered hills
- McCain Valley has loops and sand tracks — "challenging rock obstacles, pick your line carefully"
- Winter temps: 60-70°F — perfect

#### Boondocking:
- Dispersed camping available in Cleveland NF
- Lake Morena County Park nearby ($25/night, hookups available for dump/water resupply)

## 🌄 RIDGE/HILLTOP RIDING — THE EPIC VIEWS YOU WANT

This is the style you described — wide open paths along mountain ridgelines with insane panoramic views. Here's where to find it:

| Spot | Location | What Makes It Special |
|---|---|---|
| **Hungry Valley → Los Padres NF** | Gorman, CA | 2,600-8,000 ft elevation. Fire roads along ridgelines through pine forests with valley views |
| **Rowher Flat ridgelines** | Angeles NF, CA | Trails follow ridgelines with high desert panoramas. 4,000+ ft |

**For the PNW extension (April/May):**
- **Browns Camp / Tillamook State Forest, OR** — 250+ miles of trails through lush green coastal forest. Dense canopy, ferns, towering trees. Diamond Mill area has motorcycle-only technical single track. Free day use.
- **Tillamook Oregon is described as "top tier riding" by Reddit riders**

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NOTES

### BLM Rules
- 14-day stay limit within any 28-day period
- Must move 25-30 miles after limit
- Leave no trace, pack out everything
- Check fire restrictions (very common in CA)

### Arizona State Trust Land
- $15/year permit required: https://land.az.gov/recreational-permits
- Separate from BLM — look for posted signs
- Covers Vulture Peak + surrounding areas

### Alabama Hills
- Free camping but now requires a free permit
- Get online or at Eastern Sierra Visitor Center in Lone Pine

### Stark Varg Considerations
- Electric bike = silent = less likely to bother other campers/hikers
- BUT: charging on the road. Your generator + power bank should handle it, but plan charge times
- Some OHV areas may have specific rules for e-bikes vs. motorcycles — check each area

### Gear Reminders
- GPS/OnX Offroad essential everywhere
- Extra water — desert eats through it
- Spare tubes, levers, basic tools for both bikes
- Starlink = clutch for remote boondocking
- Check generator quiet hours at each spot
- Rim locks on both bikes for rocky terrain
